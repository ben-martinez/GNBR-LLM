<info type="ChemicalEntity">Tincture of Crataegus</info> (TCR), an alcoholic extract of the berries of <info type="OrganismTaxon">hawthorn</info> (<info type="OrganismTaxon">Crataegus oxycantha</info>), is used in herbal and homeopathic medicine. Pretreatment of <info type="ChemicalEntity">TCR</info>, at a dose of 0.5 mL/100 g bodyweight per day, orally for 30 days, prevented the increase in <info type="ChemicalEntity">lipid peroxidation</info> and activity of marker enzymes observed in <info type="ChemicalEntity">isoproterenol</info>-induced <info type="OrganismTaxon">rats</info> (85 mg kg(-1) s. c. for 2 days at an interval of 24 h). <info type="ChemicalEntity">TCR</info> prevented the <info type="ChemicalEntity">isoproterenol</info>-induced decrease in <info type="ChemicalEntity">antioxidant enzymes</info> in the heart and increased the rate of <info type="ChemicalEntity">ADP</info>-stimulated oxygen uptake and respiratory coupling ratio. <info type="ChemicalEntity">TCR</info> protected against pathological changes induced by <info type="ChemicalEntity">isoproterenol</info> in <info type="OrganismTaxon">rat</info> heart. The results show that pretreatment with <info type="ChemicalEntity">TCR</info> may be useful in preventing the damage induced by <info type="ChemicalEntity">isoproterenol</info> in <info type="OrganismTaxon">rat</info> heart.
